How to play Stud poker

Overview of Stud Poker Stud Poker is a popular casino table game derived from traditional five-card stud poker. Unlike standard poker games, players compete against the dealer, not against other players. There is no bluffing involved, and the outcome is decided purely by poker hand rankings. The primary objective of the game is to beat the dealer’s hand with a higher-ranking poker hand. How the Player Wins A player wins if: The player’s hand rank is higher than the dealer’s hand, or The dealer’s hand does not qualify. How the Player Loses A player loses if: The dealer’s hand rank is higher than the player’s hand, or The player folds after placing the Ante bet. Poker Hand Rankings Stud Poker follows standard poker hand rankings, from highest to lowest: Royal Flush Straight Flush Four of a Kind Full House Flush Straight Three of a Kind Two Pair One Pair High Card Game Rules The player places an Ante bet to begin the round. The dealer deals five cards face-up to the player. The dealer then deals five cards to themselves, with one card face-up and four cards face-down. After viewing their hand, the player must choose to: Call by placing an additional bet equal to 2× the Ante, or Fold, forfeiting the Ante bet. If the player calls, the dealer reveals their cards and the hands are compared. Dealer Qualification Rules For the dealer’s hand to qualify: The dealer must have Ace-King or higher. The lowest qualifying dealer hand is: A-K-4-3-2 If the Dealer Does Not Qualify The Ante bet is paid 1:1. The Call bet is pushed and returned to the player.
