How to Whitelist SportyBet Emails to Receive 2FA Codes

Unable to Receive 2FA Overview Some email domains have filters that may prevent 2FA (two-factor authentication) emails from being delivered to your inbox or may send them to your spam folder. Adding SportyBet to your email whitelist can help ensure you receive these important messages. What is a Whitelist? A whitelist is a list of approved or trusted email addresses or domains that helps your email provider determine which messages should be delivered to your inbox and not marked as spam. How to Whitelist SportyBet Emails Gmail 1. Click the gear icon in Gmail and select 'See all settings' from the Quick Settings menu. 2. Go to 'Filters and Blocked Addresses' in the top menu. 3. Select 'Create a new filter.' 4. In the 'From' field, enter @sportymailer.com to whitelist all emails from this domain. 5. Click 'Create filter.' 6. In the new window, check 'Never send it to Spam.' 7. Click 'Create filter' again to save the whitelist. Outlook 1. Click the gear icon in Outlook and select 'View all Outlook settings.' 2. Choose 'Mail' from the settings menu. 3. Select 'Junk email' from the submenu. 4. Click 'Add' under 'Safe senders and domains.' 5. Enter @sportymailer.com or the specific email address you want to whitelist. 6. Click 'Save' to update your whitelist preferences. Yahoo Mail 1. Log in to Yahoo Mail. 2. Go to 'Settings' and click 'More Settings.' 3. Select 'Filters' and click 'Add.' 4. Name the filter and add the email address you wish to whitelist. 5. Set the filter to send all mail from this address to 'Inbox.' 6. Save your settings. AOL Mail 1. Log in to your AOL account. 2. Open 'Contacts' from the left navigation pane. 3. Click the 'New Contact' icon. 4. Add the email address you want to whitelist. 5. Click the 'Add Contact' button. Note If you have tried the above solutions and still cannot receive 2FA emails, the case should be handed off to a human agent.
