Understanding Contingent Outcomes and Stake Multiplication in Bets

What are Contingent Outcomes? Definition of Contingent Outcomes Contingent outcomes occur when multiple selections are made from the same match, and the result of one selection influences or depends on the other. These selections are not independent and cannot be treated as separate events in a single combined bet. Stake Multiplication in Contingent Outcomes When more than one selection from the same match is added into a multiple bet (for example, Match Result and Total Goals), the system splits them into separate combinations. This prevents related or dependent outcomes from being part of the same bet, which would otherwise distort the odds. Example of Contingent Outcomes Selections made: - Chelsea vs Manchester United – Market Name (1X2) - Chelsea vs Manchester United – Market Name (Over 2.5 Goals) - Barcelona vs Real Madrid – Market Name (1X2) Since the first two selections are from the same match, the system creates two separate combinations: - Combination 1: Chelsea vs Manchester United (1X2) + Barcelona vs Real Madrid (1X2) - Combination 2: Chelsea vs Manchester United (Over 2.5 Goals) + Barcelona vs Real Madrid (1X2) Each combination is treated as a separate bet, so your stake is multiplied by the number of combinations (in this case, 2).
