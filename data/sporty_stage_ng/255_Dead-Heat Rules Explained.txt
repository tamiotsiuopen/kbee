Dead-Heat Rules Explained

Dead-Heat Rules Definition of a Dead-Heat A dead-heat occurs when two or more participants tie in an event or competition. This can happen in situations such as: - Two or more runners cannot be separated, even after a photo finish. - Two or more players or teams score the same number of goals or points within a tournament. How Dead-Heat Affects Bet Returns - In the event of a dead-heat, your return is divided by the number of participants that have tied. - Example: If three runners finish tied for 1st place in a race, you will receive one third (1/3) of the return for the bet you placed.
