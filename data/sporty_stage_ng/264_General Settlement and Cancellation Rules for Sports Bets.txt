General Settlement and Cancellation Rules for Sports Bets

General Settlement and Cancellation Rules Outcome Verification and Settlement Delays - Settlement of bets may be delayed or voided if the outcome of a real sports event cannot be officially confirmed. Known Outcomes - Bets placed when the outcome is already known may be voided. Incorrect Prices - Bets with incorrect or clearly miscalculated prices, such as a 100 percent deviation from the market average, may be voided. Abandoned Coverage - If coverage is interrupted but the match finishes regularly, bets will be settled based on the final result. Incorrect Settlements - Mistaken settlements can be corrected at any time. Unusual Match Formats - If the match format or rules deviate from accepted norms, related markets may be voided. Incomplete Matches - For unplayed or incomplete matches (such as those affected by disqualification, interruption, or withdrawal), undecided markets will be voided.
