Glossary of American Sports Betting Terms

American Sports Betting Terms Overview This section defines common American sports betting terms to help you understand how various types of bets work. Definitions of Common Terms Money Line - The odds for a team to win a match outright. A tie is not included in this type of bet. Spread - A handicap applied to a team's score for betting purposes. The specified number of points is added to or subtracted from that team's final score at the end of the match. - Example: If you place a -10.5 bet on a team in the NBA, you need to deduct 10.5 from that team's score after the game. If the game finishes 111-100, the adjusted score is 100.5-100, and your bet would win. Point Spread / Puck Line / Run Line - These are handicap betting markets specific to American sports. - Point Spread: Used in Basketball and American Football. - Puck Line: Used in Ice Hockey. - Run Line: Used in Baseball. - You select a team and choose from available spreads, such as -5.5 or +2.5. Price - Also known as Odds, Line, or Point Handicap. - The price is used to calculate your winnings and reflects how likely an outcome is. Push - Occurs when a game is tied after the handicap or total is taken into consideration. - In this case, your stake is refunded. - Example: If you bet on a -5.0 spread and your team wins by exactly 5 points, the adjusted score is a tie and your stake is refunded. Parlay - An accumulator bet involving two or more selections. - All selections must win for the bet to receive a return. - Example: An American Football doubles bet on the Dolphins and Chiefs to win requires both predictions to be correct for the bet to win.
