Understanding Asian Handicap Betting

Asian Handicap Overview Asian Handicaps are a type of soccer market where you place a handicap bet on a specific team. A variety of Asian Handicaps are available under Asian Lines within selected fixtures. Whole Line Asian Handicap - When you place a -1.0 Whole Line Asian Handicap bet on a team, one goal is deducted from that team's final score after the match. - Example: If your team wins 2-0, after applying the -1.0 handicap, the result is 1-0 and your bet wins. - If the match ends in a draw after the handicap is applied, the bet is void and your stake is refunded. - If the team you bet on wins by exactly one goal, the result after the handicap is a draw and your stake is refunded. - If the match result is a draw before the handicap, your bet loses. In-Play Asian Handicap Bets - For In-Play Asian Handicap bets, only goals scored after the bet is placed are considered for settlement. - The scoreline is treated as 0-0 at the time of bet placement. - The odds and available lines are adjusted to reflect this. Half Line Asian Handicap (e.g. +0.5) - Half Line Asian Handicaps eliminate the possibility of a draw after the handicap is applied. - Example: If you place a +0.5 Asian Handicap bet and the match ends 1-1, the amended result is 1.5-1 in your team's favor, so your bet wins. Split Line (Quarter Line) Asian Handicap (e.g. -1.0, -1.5) - Stakes on Split Line Asian Handicaps are divided equally between the Whole Line and Half Line Asian Handicaps. - Example: A bet on -1.25 is split, with half the stake on -1.0 and half on -1.5.
