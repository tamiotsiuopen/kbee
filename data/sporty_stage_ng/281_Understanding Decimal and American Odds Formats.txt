Understanding Decimal and American Odds Formats

What are Decimal and American Odds? Overview This article explains the differences between Decimal Odds and American Odds, including how to interpret and calculate potential returns for each format. Decimal Odds - Decimal odds show the total return for each unit bet, including the original stake. - To calculate the potential return, multiply your stake by the decimal odds offered. - Example: If you bet 100 units at odds of 2.270, your total return is 100 x 2.270 = 227 units. This includes your original 100-unit stake and 127 units of profit. American Odds - American odds indicate either how much you can win (positive odds) or how much you need to bet (negative odds) to win a certain amount. Positive American Odds (+) - Example: +200 - Shows how much profit you would make on a 100-unit stake. - A +200 line means if you bet 100 units, you win 200 units profit, plus your original 100-unit stake back. - Total return: 300 units. - Positive odds indicate an underdog. Negative American Odds (−) - Example: −150 - Shows how much you need to bet to win 100 units of profit. - A −150 line means you need to bet 150 units to win 100 units profit, and you receive your 150-unit stake back as well. - Total return: 250 units. - Negative odds indicate a favourite.
