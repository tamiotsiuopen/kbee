Understanding Rule 4 Deductions in Betting

Rule 4 Deductions Overview Rule 4 is an industry standard deduction applied to winnings when a participant is withdrawn from an event after bets have been placed. Purpose of Rule 4 - Bookmakers offer odds based on all participants being present in an event. - If a participant is withdrawn after bets are placed, the event's competitiveness decreases, so a deduction is applied to reflect this change. How Rule 4 Deductions Work - A quoted deduction is applied for every 1.00 unit of currency won. - The deduction applies to all bets placed before the withdrawal of a participant. - The size of the deduction depends on the odds of the withdrawn participant at the time of withdrawal. - The shorter the odds, the larger the Rule 4 deduction.
