Common Reasons Players Lose Bets in Aviator Timing Failures (Not Cashing Out on Time) - Losses often occur when players fail to cash out before the plane crashes. - Example: If the plane reaches a 1.5x multiplier and you wait for 2x, but the plane crashes at 1.7x, you lose your bet. Same Cash-Out Odds or Coefficient - The multiplier at which the plane crashes is called the coefficient (e.g., 1.5x, 2x, 10x). - If you set your cash-out odds exactly at the multiplier where the plane crashes, your bet is settled as lost. - Example: If the plane flies away at 1.5x and you set your cash-out at 1.5x, you lose the bet. Your cash-out odds should always be less than the crash multiplier (e.g., 1.49x or lower).
