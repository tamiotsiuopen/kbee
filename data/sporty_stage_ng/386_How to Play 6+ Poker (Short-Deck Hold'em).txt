Rules - Player receives two face-up cards; Dealer receives two face-down cards. - The Board is dealt face-up. - After all cards are dealt, the Dealer's cards are revealed. - Results are determined based on video stream and scanner data. - The goal is to form the best five-card combination using pocket and community cards. The side with the highest combination wins. - In case of a tie, winnings are paid to all players who bet on the split. Possible Poker Combinations (Lowest to Highest) 1. High Card: No combination; highest card wins. 2. Pair: Two cards of the same rank. Higher pair or kicker wins if tied. 3. Two Pair: Two sets of pairs. Higher pair or kicker wins if tied. 4. Straight: Five consecutive cards of different suits. Aces can be high or low. 5. Three of a Kind: Three cards of the same rank. Higher set or kicker wins if tied. 6. Full House: Three of a kind plus a pair. Higher three of a kind or pair wins if tied. 7. Flush: Five non-consecutive cards of the same suit. Higher pocket card(s) wins if tied. 8. Four of a Kind: Four cards of the same rank. Higher set or kicker wins if tied. 9. Straight Flush: Five consecutive cards of the same suit. Higher straight flush wins if tied. 10. Royal Flush: 10 to Ace, all the same suit. Strongest hand. Gameplay Sequence 1. First Betting Round: Begins before cards are dealt. Players can bet on available outcomes. 2. Second Betting Round: After dealing two face-up cards to Player and two face-down cards to Dealer. Odds are updated. Bets from previous rounds do not affect new bets. 3. Third Betting Round: Dealer discards one card, then deals the flop (three community cards) face-up. Odds updated. Players can bet again. 4. Fourth Betting Round: Dealer discards one card, deals the turn (fourth community card) face-up. Odds updated. New bets accepted. 5.
