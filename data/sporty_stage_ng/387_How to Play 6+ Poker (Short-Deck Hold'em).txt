End of Draw: After the fifth betting round, Dealer's hand is revealed and winnings are paid. Card Shuffling - Two decks are used: one for dealing, one for shuffling during the draw. - After each draw, used cards are returned to the deck, which is shuffled for the next draw. - A newly shuffled deck is used for each draw. Betting Limits - Maximum and minimum betting limits are set by the gambling company. Special Cases Cancelled Draws - Draws may be cancelled if: - The scanner cannot scan a card or readings do not match. - Cards are misplaced or mixed. - Technical problems occur (internet, equipment, or dealer error). - Dealer errors include: - Altered dealing sequence - Marked/damaged cards - Cards fall off the table or are not visible - Cards turned face-up due to incorrect shuffling - Dealer forgets to use the cut card - Incorrect card scanning - If a draw is cancelled, players receive a refund (odds set to 1.00). Card Burning - A card may be burned if shown face-up before the betting round ends. Card Burning Procedure: 1. Dealer shows the card face-up to all players. 2. Dealer announces the card will be burned. 3. Dealer places the burned card to their right side (left side on screen). 4. Dealer draws a new card from the shoe to replace the burned card. Game Organization - The game runs 24/7 with maintenance breaks as needed. Dealer and card changes are broadcast live. Equipment Used - 36-card short deck (6 to Ace), each with a unique barcode. - Cut card. - Table with printed boxes for Player, Dealer, and Board cards. - Integrated table card scanner. - Integrated card shuffling machine. - Shoe with shuffled cards.
