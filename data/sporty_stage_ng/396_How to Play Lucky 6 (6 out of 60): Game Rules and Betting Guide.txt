How to Play Lucky 6 (6 out of 60): Game Rules and Betting Guide

 device used to mix and randomly select winning balls. - Balls: Set of 60 balls (30 red, 30 blue), numbered 0 to 9. Total sum of all numbers is 270 (red balls: 138, blue balls: 132). - Drum: Part of the game machine where balls are mixed and drawn. - Cylinders: Six identical parts connected to the drum, where winning balls are drawn and displayed. - Mixing and Selection process: Starts when all balls fall into the drum; ends when all 6 winning balls are selected into the cylinders. - Odds: Numerical value set by the organizer, multiplied by the stake to calculate winnings. - Odd numbers: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9. - Even numbers: 0, 2, 4, 6, 8. Rules - Six winning balls are randomly drawn into the cylinders. - The draw is considered complete when the Mixing and Selection process is over. - If the process is incomplete, the draw is void or continued until six balls are selected. - The order of winning balls is determined by cylinder position (left to right); the order of filling does not affect results. Gameplay - Only one betting round per draw. - Betting round starts immediately after the previous draw ends and lasts about four minutes. Betting Limits - Maximum and minimum betting limits are set by the gambling company. Special Cases Cancelled Draws - Draws may be cancelled due to technical failures (internet, studio, presenter mistakes). - Presenter mistakes leading to cancellation include: - Balls not prepared before the draw starts. - Balls not visible on screen due to presenter actions. Continuation of Mixing and Selection - Mixing and Selection process may be continued if: - Fewer than 6 balls are selected into the cylinders when the process ends. - Selected balls fall back into the drum during the process. Video and Audio Streaming Issues - If audio is lost or the presenter announces the wrong numbers, the video broadcast determines the correct outcome. - If a player cannot see the live broadcast due to personal technical issues but the broadcast is available in the archive, the draw is considered to have taken place.
