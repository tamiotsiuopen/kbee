Game Rules Definitions - Wheel of Fortune: A round gaming device divided into nineteen sectors by metal holders. - Sector: One of nineteen parts of the wheel, numbered 1 to 18 or marked with a special symbol (a cup with a star). All sectors are equal in size. - Draw result: The sector where the lower end of the pointer stops (closer to the center of the wheel), determined by fixed rules. - Pointer: The part at the top middle of the wheel that determines the result. - Odds: The numerical value multiplied by the player's stake to calculate winnings. Gameplay Rules - The presenter spins the wheel as described above. - Only one Wheel of Fortune is used per game. - Only one spin per draw unless a repeat is required. - The draw is valid if the wheel makes at least three full spins clockwise. - The spin starts when the pointer leaves its previous sector. - The result is the sector where the pointer lands after the wheel stops. - If the pointer stops between sectors on a metal handle and does not fully jump over, the sector on the right side of the handle is the correct result. - There is only one betting round per draw, lasting about two minutes, starting immediately after the previous draw. Betting Limits - Maximum and minimum betting limits are determined by the gambling company. Special Cases Cancelled Draws - Draws can be cancelled due to technical failures, such as internet or studio issues, or presenter mistakes. - Presenter mistakes that can result in cancelled draws include: - The wheel does not make at least three full spins during a repeated spin. - The presenter touches the wheel or changes its speed while it is spinning. Repeated Spin - A spin must be repeated if the wheel does not make three full spins from the start until it stops.
