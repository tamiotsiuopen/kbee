What to Do If Your Account Is Compromised

My Account is Compromised Overview This guide explains the steps to temporarily deactivate your account if you believe it has been compromised, and how to restore it once the issue is resolved. How to Temporarily Deactivate Your Account 1. Enter your registered mobile number. 2. Choose the option to Deactivate. 3. Specify the reason for deactivation as 'Fraud and/or Account Compromised'. 4. Click on the Next button. 5. To claim account ownership, select your preferred method from the options on the screen to receive your OTP. Note: To use the Telegram option, you must have an active Telegram account linked to your phone number. 6. Enter the 6-digit OTP code received via your chosen method. Result - Your account will be temporarily deactivated. - You will be logged out of all existing devices. How to Restore Your Account - Follow the same steps as above, but select the Reactivate option and complete the process. Important Notes - Once an account is created with a verified phone number, the number cannot be changed to a different one. - If you have lost your SIM, contact your mobile network provider and request a new SIM with the same number.
